# Co-Goods Manifesto

*This is a placeholder. The full manifesto will be drafted in the open and
refined through community contribution.*

We are building Co-Goods as an open-innovation initiative: the protocol
layer for goods that get better as more people use them together —
antirival goods, in the academic sense. Physical products designed and
produced collectively, with a shared fabric of design, manufacturing, and
value distribution.

The work is open. Findings live in a public content repository; the
protocol's vocabulary, mechanics, and decisions are drafted publicly.
This manifesto is the foundational statement of what we believe, why it
matters, and how we plan to make it real.

## What we believe (placeholder)

- That value created together can be distributed back to those who helped
  create it, fairly and durably.
- That the rules of how communities co-produce can be made legible and
  composable.
- That "ownership" is plural, and that protocols can make plural
  ownership work in practice.

## Why now (placeholder)

The tooling for community-led economic coordination has matured enough
that physical-product networks can be governed transparently. The gap is
in protocol design — the shared rules that let many brands, communities,
and contributors co-good without locking themselves into one platform.

## How we work (placeholder)

In the open. Every observation, insight, and hypothesis is published as
we develop it. Essays argue our take; reports compile findings formally.
Contributors welcome — see [our contribution guide](/contributing).
